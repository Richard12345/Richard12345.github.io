Six Sigma

 

What is Six Sigma ?

  • Customer focus data driven improvement process.
  • Statistical Six Sigma means about 3.4 defects per million opportunities.

What does Six Sigma mean for the implementer ?

  • Increase customer satisfaction.
  • Increase profit margin by reducing internal cost.
  • Provide quantifiable results of improvement that can be accounted.

Steps of Six Sigma:

  1. Define
  2. Measure
  3. Analyze
  4. Improve
  5. Control

Tools/Techniques  (Non-Exhaustive):

  1. Process Mapping
  2. Descriptive statistics.
  3. Charting: Control chart, Scatter Diagram, Cause/Effect, Pareto, Run.
  4. D.P.M.O (Defects Per Million Opportunity) and Yield.
  5. Process Capability Analysis.
  6. Measurement System Analysis (M.S.A)
  7. Quality Function Deployment.
  8. Failure Mode Effect Analysis (F.M.E.A) / Control Plan.
  9. Confidence Interval and Hypothesis Testing
  10. Analysis of variance. (ANOVA)
  11. Variance Component.
  12. Design of Experiment. (D.O.E)
  13. Regression.
